This view shows the different level of confidences attributed to the different elements of an evidence tuple (i.e. a set of data concerning a place, religion, time span and the respective confidences).

Contents

The view consists of a table in which each row represents one level of certainty and each column an aspect of confidence. The rows are sorted by descending level of confidence.

Levels of Confidence

Generally, the confidence of any given information was categorized according to these 5 degrees: certain (blue), probable, contested, uncertain, and false (red). In addition, a confidence can have no value at all (green). The attribution with "no value" is used while data is still being collected and usually does not appear in a published instance of this visualiziation. A notable exception are places, especially regions, with no exact geo-coordinates. In that case, no attribution of confidence except "no value" is suitable. The number of evidence tuples with the respective confidence level is given as a number in the respective column.

Aspects of Confidence

Time span confidence
signifies the confidence in the veracity of the time interval stored in an evidence tuple. Evidence tuples without a time attribute are assigned the lowest level of confidence.

Location confidence
signifies the confidence in the geographical location of a city.
Place attribution confidence
signifies the confidence in assigning a location from the database to a toponym from a historical source.

Source confidence
signifies the confidence in the veracity and objectivity of the historical source itself regarding a piece of evidence.
Interpretation confidence
signifies the confidence in the correct interpretation and recording of the source when entering a piece of evidence into the database.

Religion confidence
signifies the confidence in the veracity of the presence of a particular religious group to an evidence tuple.

Interaction

For each aspect of confidence and each confidence level, a checkbox is shown in the respective cell. Unchecking a checkbox will filter out evidence tuples with that confidence level for that aspect of confidence. This allows for a very fine-grained control over what data to visualize. Note that the selection is not applied until the Apply button is clicked. This button is disabled if the selected filters are up to date with the visualized data.

The filters work in logical conjunction across different aspects of confidence; that is, having filters in place for multiple aspects of confidence will only show evidence tuples that match all filters.

Three additional buttons in the top left corner make filtering more convenient:

Uncheck all
Uncheck all checkboxes.
Invert
Invert all checkboxes. Checked boxes are unchecked, unchecked boxes are checked.
Check all
Check all checkboxes.
Default
Revert the selection of checkboxes to the default. By default, all confidence levels are selected for location confidence, and only certain and probable for the other confidence aspects.

In addition, entire rows or columns of checkboxes can be set to either checked or unchecked by clicking on the row header (the confidence levels) or the column header (the aspects of confidence). Here, the new value for the new row (or column) is the inverse of what the majority of values (3 or more) was before; for example, if 4 checkboxes were checked, and 2 unchecked, for the uncertain row, clicking on the row header would uncheck all checkboxes in the row.

Visualized Aspect of Confidence

By clicking on the eye in the bottom row of each column, the aspect of confidence that is visualized in Confidence Mode (cf. "Display mode" in the Settings view) can be selected. The currently selected aspect is indicated by a colored eye. By default, religion confidence is used.
