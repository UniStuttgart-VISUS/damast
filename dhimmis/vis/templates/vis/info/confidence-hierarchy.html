This view shows the different level of confidences attributed to different data of an evidence tuple (i.e., a set of data concerning a place, religion, time span and the respective confidences).

Contents

The view consists of a table in which each row represents one level of certainty and each column a certain category of data. The rows are sorted by descending level of certainty.

Levels of Confidence

Generally, the confidence of any given information was categorized according to these 5 degrees: false (red), uncertain, contested, probable, and certain (blue). In addition, a confidence can have no value at all (green), e.g. when ###.

Each level of confidence is represented by one line in the controls. The number of evidence tuples with the respective confidence level is given as a number in the respective column.

Aspects of Confidence

Time span confidence signifies the confidence in the veracity of the time interval stored in an evidence tuple. Evidence tuples without a time attribute are assigned the lowest level of confidence.

Location confidence signifies the confidence in the geographical location of a city. Place attribution confidence signifies the confidence in assigning a location from the database to a toponym from a historical source. Note the difference in these two kind of confidences regarding places.

Source confidence signifies the confidence in the veracity and objectivity of the historical source of a piece of evidence. ###explain Interpretation confidence signifies the confidence in the interpretation of the source when entering a piece of historical evidence into the database. ###explain

Religion confidence is the confidence in the veracity of a piece of evidence regarding the religion. ###explain

Interaction

For each aspect of confidence and each confidence level, a checkbox is shown in the respective cell. Unchecking a checkbox will filter out evidence tuples with that confidence level for that aspect of confidence. This allows for a very fine-grained control over what data to visualize.

The filters are conjunctive across different aspects of confidence; that is, having filters in place for multiple aspects of confidence will only show evidence tuples that match all filters. Filtering will happen only after the Apply button is pressed. That button is disabled if the selected filters are up to date with the visualized data.

Three additional buttons in the top left corner make filtering more convenient:

Uncheck all
Uncheck all checkboxes.
Invert
Invert all checkboxes. Checked boxes are unchecked, unchecked boxes are checked.
Check all
Check all checkboxes.

In addition, entire rows or columns of checkboxes can be set to either checked or unchecked by clicking on the row header (the confidence levels) or the column header (the aspects of confidence). Here, the new value for the new row (or column) is the inverse of what the majority of values (3 or more) was before; for example, if 4 checkboxes were selected, and 2 deselected, for the uncertain row, clicking on the row header would uncheck all checkboxes in the row.

Visualized Aspect of Confidence

By clicking on the eye in the bottom row of each column, the aspect of confidence that is visualized in Confidence Mode (cf. "Display mode" in the Settings view) can be selected. The currently selected aspect is indicated by a colored eye. By default, religion confidence is used.
