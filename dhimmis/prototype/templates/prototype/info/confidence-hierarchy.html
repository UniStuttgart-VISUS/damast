Aspects of Confidence

Time confidence signifies the confidence in the veracity of the time interval stored in an evidence tuple. Evidence tuples without a time attribute are assigned the lowest level of confidence.

Location confidence signifies the confidence in the geographical location of a city. Place attribution confidence signifies the confidence in assigning a location from the database to a toponym from a historical source.

Source confidence signifies the confidence in the veracity and objectivity of the historical source of a piece of evidence. Interpretation confidence signifies the confidence in the interpretation of the source when entering a piece of historical evidence into the database.

Religion confidence is the confidence in the veracity of a piece of evidence regarding the religion.

Levels of Confidence

Currently, five levels of confidence are used for the data: false (red), uncertain, contested, probable, and certain (blue). Data with a missing confidence value is shown in green.

Each level of confidence is represented by one line in the controls. The number of evidence tuples with the respective confidence level is given as a number in the respective column.

Filtering

For each aspect of confidence and each confidence level, a checkbox is shown in the respective cell. Deselecting a checkbox will filter out evidence tuples with that confidence level for that aspect of confidence. This allows for a very fine-grained control over what data to visualize.

The filters are conjunctive across different aspects of confidence; that is, having filters in place for multiple aspects of confidence will only show evidence tuples that match all filters. Filtering will happen only after the Apply button is pressed. That button is disabled if the selected filters are up to date with the visualized data.

Toggling Multiple Checkboxes

Instead of toggling each checkbox individually, some shortcut controls are provided to facilitate quicker selection. In the header (top left of the view), three buttons provide functionality that applies to all checkboxes at once:
  • Deselect all checkboxes at once
  • Invert all checkboxes (i.e., selected ones become deselected, and vice versa)
  • Select all checkboxes at once
In addition, entire rows or columns of checkboxes can be set to either selected or deselected by clicking on the row header (the confidence levels) or the column header (the aspects of confidence). Here, the new value for the new row (or column) is the inverse of what the majority of values (3 or more) was before; for example, if four checkboxes were selected, and two deselected, for the uncertain row, clicking on the row header would deselect all checkboxes in the row.

Visualized Aspect of Confidence

By clicking on the eye in the bottom row of each column, the aspect of confidence that is visualized in Confidence Mode can be selected. The currently selected aspect is indicated by a colored eye. By default, the religion confidence is used.
